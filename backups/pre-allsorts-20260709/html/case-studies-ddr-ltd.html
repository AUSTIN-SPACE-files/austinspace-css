The challenge

Where things stood

DDR had been operating under ddrdecorators.co.uk since 2019, built by a third party and untouched since. In the six years that followed, the business had grown considerably: a 20-strong certified team, major MoD contracts, BM Trada accreditation, and partnerships with DIO, Vinci and VIVO. None of it appeared anywhere on the website.

Technical issues

  • Logo missing or broken across all pages
  • No mobile optimisation
  • No SEO foundation — no meta titles, descriptions or alt tags
  • No analytics or performance tracking in place

Functional gaps

  • Three pages only: Home, Case Studies, Contact
  • No certifications displayed anywhere
  • No structured quotation process
  • No GDPR-compliant forms or privacy policy

The old site

Brand & content issues

  • Rebrand from DDR Decorators Ltd to DDR LTD underway, new URL needed
  • Services listed as three generic painting categories only
  • Defence sector work completely absent despite being core to the business
  • No mention of MoD, DIO, Vinci or VIVO partnerships
  • No team, no values, no company identity
The solution

How it was built

Discovery

Understanding the Business

  • DDR came with a slideshow of layout ideas, a starting point, not a specification
  • Full scope agreed through conversation: 14 pages across Homepage, Divisions, Our Customers, Case Studies, About Us, Community Projects, Testimonials, Contact and policy pages
  • Domain migration from ddrdecorators.co.uk to ddr-ltd.co.uk scoped in from the start
  • Content requirements mapped per page before any design work began
Design

Visual Direction & Layout

  • Existing red and black brand colours retained and built into a clean, consistent design system
  • New DDR LTD logo integrated throughout
  • Page layouts built to surface MoD partnerships, BM Trada accreditation, the full range of divisions, and real client testimonials — consistently and prominently
  • Mobile-first approach throughout
Build

Full Squarespace Development

  • 14 pages built in full, with eight service divisions each given a dedicated card
  • Three individual case study pages (Hyde Park Barracks, Regents Park Barracks and Territorial House), each with project details, images, challenge and outcome
  • Our Customers section giving dedicated, credible space to DIO, Vinci and VIVO
  • Structured quotation form and named team contacts replacing the old basic email box
  • Review submission functionality added for ongoing testimonial collection
Launch

Go-Live & Handover

  • Domain migrated from ddrdecorators.co.uk to ddr-ltd.co.uk with redirects in place
  • Google Search Console connected and sitemap submitted
  • CookieYes integrated for GDPR compliance
  • SEO titles, meta descriptions and image alt tags applied across all pages
  • Handover call arranged so the team could manage the site confidently from day one

FAQ

Common questions

How do you handle a client who already has strong ideas about what they want?

That's actually the best starting point. DDR came with a detailed slideshow, a clear sense of their brand direction, and specific functionality requests. The job is to take that vision and execute it properly: filling the gaps, advising where needed, and delivering something that looks and works better than what was sketched out.

DDR operates in the defence sector. How do you make that credible on a website?

Through clarity and confidence. MoD credentials, certifications, and a track record of specialist work need to be front and centre, not buried. The site was structured to lead with authority, with a clean professional aesthetic that reflects the seriousness of the sector.

What does a 14-page build involve in practice?

Every page planned, designed, and built to a consistent standard, with all the content organised, a clear navigation structure, and the Squarespace technical foundation in place from day one. For DDR that meant migrating to a new domain, building out their full service offering, and making the site work as a credible business tool for the first time.

How quickly can a Squarespace project like this be turned around?

The DDR project ran from discovery to launch in six weeks. Timeline depends on content readiness and complexity. Having a clear brief and assets ready makes a significant difference to how quickly things move.
